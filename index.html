History

The history of the sport can be traced back in India, but invented/adopted by British officers. Games involving shuttlecocks have been around before, but the first ever game of what can be considered badminton was played but those British officers. The name Badminton was named after the Duke of Beaufort's Badminton House, Badminton being a name of a village name.

Rules

A round consists of rallies. A round is won after a player earns 21 points. If both players reach 20 points, then the target is raised to 22. This continues on until 29-29, then in this case it's first to score 30. Best out of three rounds.
To score points, the shuttlecock must land within the opponent's court. If the shuttlecock lands outside, then the opponent scores a point.
At a start of a rally, the players stand diagonally of each other. The server stands the left service court if their score is odd, and on the right if even. After the rally is won by the server, the server serves again now from the other side. If the server loses the rally, the opponent then serves and goes and serves from the correct side according to their score.

how to stand properly. There are different stances for attacking, defending, and playing near the net. Attacking stance involves facing body sidewards, raising both arms, then swinging to hit the shuttlecock. Defending involves keeping the body low, the racket waist height. Net stance needs you to step forward with one foot and extend your arm from the same side.

The court is small, so maximizing movement is necessary. From the starting position (center of your court) you can only move 2-3 steps forwards, 2-3 steps backwards, and 1 step to the sides

Service is the first step in starting a rally. There are two serves, high serve and low serve. High serve tries to hit the back of the opponent's court the low serve the front.

Almost indefensible move in badminton. Forehand smash is like throwing a ball, backhand smash has you looking away from the net and hitting the shuttlecock with your backhand, and jumping smash is a forehand smash but executed mid-air.

These moves try to exploit the opponent's need to try and catch the shuttlecock. Dropshot tries to aim for the front, while the lob aims for the back.

Smash

Smash is usually a finisher move in badminton. It is the act of violently sending the shuttlecock speeding through air, away from your opponent. Because of it's near indefensibility, this is a very important skill to know. Muscles needed for the skills includes the upper back, forearms, shoulders, lower back
These exercises all try to focus the arms, back, and core, all muscle groups needed to deliver a deadly smash. So by exercising those muscles, one can develop their smash to greatness.

grip

The grip is the proper hold of the racket. The thumb on the wide side, and the rest gripping the handle. This is important because it can reduce injuries, allows for greater range of motion, more efficient hits, and stops your racket from flying away. That last one something that happened (still does) that I remember very vividly. Something that can be fixed with a towel grip, but something that a good grip strength and proper grip technique can help.
A lot of the exercise here focuses on the wrist, but some of them also focuses on the arms. Good grip strength is important for a proper badminton grip. So by developing grip strength, we also improve our badminton grip. Though, even if a good grip can only truly be developed with actual practice, increasing grip strength is still a good idea. Same with the arms strength.
